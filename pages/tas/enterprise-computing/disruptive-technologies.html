Disruptive Technologies

Disruptive technologies are innovations that significantly alter the way consumers, industries, or businesses operate. These technologies displace established products and technology, potentially creating new industries or revolutionizing existing ones.

Digital photography uses cameras containing arrays of electronic photodetectors to produce images focused by a lens, as opposed to an exposure on photographic film. These captured images are stored as a computer file ready for further digital processing, viewing, digital publishing, or printing.

Personal computers (PCs) are versatile devices that can be used for various tasks, including word processing, internet browsing, gaming, and much more. PCs typically come with an operating system (e.g., Windows, macOS) and can run a wide range of software applications.

Ride-sharing services like Uber and Lyft allow users to book a ride using a mobile app. These services connect passengers with drivers of vehicles for hire and have transformed the transportation industry by providing a convenient and often cheaper alternative to traditional taxis.

Streaming services like Netflix, Hulu, and Amazon Prime Video allow users to watch TV shows and movies on-demand over the internet. These services have disrupted traditional cable and satellite TV by offering more flexibility and often lower costs.
